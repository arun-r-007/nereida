Understanding Marine Life

Discover how cutting-edge science is helping us track and protect marine biodiversity.

Threats to Marine Life

Life in the sea is threatened by global warming, ocean acidification, pollution, and over-harvesting of marine resources. Monitoring these changes is essential for sustainable conservation efforts.

Environmental DNA (eDNA)

All forms of marine life leave traces of DNA in seawater. By analyzing these eDNA samples, scientists can detect species from microbes to mammals without direct observation.


MBON and MBARI Collaboration

Innovative methods are transforming how we study marine ecosystems at a global scale.

Marine Biodiversity Observation Network (MBON)

MBON, led by MBARI and collaborators, uses the Monterey Bay National Marine Sanctuary as a model to improve biodiversity assessments and marine data sharing.

Autonomous Technologies

Using autonomous vehicles and advanced sampling techniques, scientists can now observe marine ecosystems at unprecedented scales, helping protect marine life more effectively.
